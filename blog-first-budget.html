How to Create Your First Budget with Your Monthly Salary

October 24, 2025 • EZer Financial Team • 6 min read • Personal Finance

Why Budgeting is Your Financial GPS

Imagine driving from Mumbai to Delhi without a map or GPS. That's what managing money without a budget feels like. Many working Indians don’t keep a written budget, yet those who do typically save and plan more consistently.

"A budget is telling your money where to go instead of wondering where it went."
— Dave Ramsey, Financial Expert

Step 1: Calculate Your After-Tax Monthly Income

What to include: Your salary after PF, taxes, and deductions. For freelancers, calculate average monthly income over the last 6 months.

Example: Amit earns ₹50,000 gross. After ₹3,500 PF and ₹2,000 tax = ₹44,500 take-home.

Step 2: The 50/30/20 Rule (Adapted for India)

This globally-proven formula works brilliantly for Indian salaries:

  • 50% on Needs (Rent, groceries, utilities, EMI, insurance, transport)
  • 30% on Wants (Dining out, entertainment, shopping, hobbies, travel)
  • 20% on Savings & Investments (Emergency fund, mutual funds, PPF, retirement)

For Amit (₹44,500 take-home):

  • Needs: ₹22,250
  • Wants: ₹13,350
  • Savings: ₹8,900
"Do not save what is left after spending, but spend what is left after saving."

Step 4: Track Your 'Wants' Wisely

This is where most people overspend. Common wants for Indians:

  • Dining out / Food delivery (₹3,000-5,000)
  • Movies / Entertainment (₹1,000-2,000)
  • Shopping (clothes, gadgets) (₹2,000-4,000)
  • Subscriptions (Netflix, Prime, etc.) (₹500-1,000)
  • Weekend outings (₹2,000-3,000)

Pro Tip: The 30% wants category is flexible. Some months you might spend 35%, others 25%. The key is averaging 30% over 3-6 months.

Common Indian Budget Challenges (And Solutions)

Challenge 1: "I live in an expensive city. 50% isn't enough for needs!"

Solution: Adjust to 60/25/15 temporarily. As income grows, shift back to 50/30/20.

Challenge 2: "My income varies every month (freelancer/business owner)."

Solution: Budget based on your lowest monthly income. Treat extras as bonus savings.

Challenge 3: "I support my parents. How do I budget?"

Solution: Count parental support as a 'Need'. Adjust percentages: maybe 55/25/20.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 50/30/20 budgeting rule?

The 50/30/20 rule allocates 50% of your income to needs (rent, groceries, utilities), 30% to wants (entertainment, dining out), and 20% to savings and investments. It's a simple framework that works well for Indian salaries.

How do I calculate my take-home salary?

Subtract PF contributions (typically 12%) and income tax from your gross salary. For example, if you earn ₹50,000 gross with ₹3,500 PF and ₹2,000 tax, your take-home is ₹44,500.

Can I adjust the 50/30/20 rule for expensive cities?

Yes! If you live in expensive cities like Mumbai or Bangalore, you can adjust to 60/25/15 temporarily. The key is to gradually work towards 50/30/20 as your income grows.
