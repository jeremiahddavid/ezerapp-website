5 Common Money Mistakes Indians Make (And How EZer Fixes Them)

November 15, 2025 • EZer Financial Team • 5 min read • Personal Finance

The Hidden Cost of Financial Negligence

According to LocalCircles' 2024 national household earnings and savings outlook survey, 48% of Indian households expect up to 25% dip in earnings in 2025, while 45% expect up to 25% dip in savings. This alarming trend reveals a broader crisis: Indian household savings have dropped to a multi-decadal low, with net financial savings standing at around 5.3% of GDP in FY 23.

The survey reveals that household borrowings rose to 5.8% of GDP, marking the highest levels since the 1970s. Rising household debt, driven by increased retail borrowing such as personal loans and credit card usage, has restricted consumer spending significantly.

"Indians are great savers, but often we save what's left after spending. Instead, spend what's left after saving."
— Warren Buffett (quoted by PM Narendra Modi in Mann Ki Baat)

Mistake #1: Not Tracking Small UPI Payments

The Problem: That ₹50 chai, ₹100 lunch, or ₹200 auto ride seems insignificant. But these small UPI transactions add up quickly.

The Numbers: If you spend just ₹200 daily on "small" expenses, that's ₹6,000 monthly or ₹72,000 yearly!

According to the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation's Household Consumption Expenditure Survey 2023-24, rural households spent more on conveyance (7.59%), medical expenses (6.83%), and clothing (6.63%), while urban households spent more on conveyance (8.46%), miscellaneous goods (6.92%), and rent (6.58%).

How EZer Fixes This: Our automatic SMS parsing captures every UPI transaction from GPay, PhonePe, PayTM, and more. You'll see exactly where your money goes without lifting a finger.

Example: Priya from Bangalore

Priya, a 28-year-old software engineer, was shocked when EZer showed her spending ₹12,000 monthly on food delivery and coffee runs. "I had no idea! I thought it was maybe ₹4,000-5,000," she said. After seeing the data, she cut down to ₹6,000 monthly, saving ₹72,000 in a year - enough for a Thailand vacation!

Mistake #2: Missing EMI and Credit Card Due Dates

The Problem: Late payment fees, interest charges, and credit score damage. According to recent credit analysis, even a 30-day delay in bill payments can cause your CIBIL score to drop by 100 points.

The Cost: Average late fee: ₹500-1,000 per card. For someone with 2-3 credit cards, that's ₹24,000+ wasted annually!

"Your credit score is your financial reputation. Missing even a single payment can impact your credit score negatively, and it may take months or even years to recover."
— Paisabazaar Credit Analysis

According to Tata Capital's research, late payments are reported to credit bureaus and remain in your credit report for at least three years. Multiple instances of missed payments indicate indisciplined credit behavior and pose a higher risk of default.

How EZer Fixes This: Smart bill reminders notify you 3 days before, 1 day before, and on the due date. You'll never miss another payment.

Mistake #3: Paying for Unused Subscriptions

The Problem: Netflix, Amazon Prime, Hotstar, Spotify, gym memberships, magazine subscriptions... Do you actually use them all?

The Numbers: Studies suggest that Indian households pay for an average of 4-5 subscriptions but actively use only 2-3. That's ₹500-1,500 monthly down the drain!

Example: The Sharma Family from Delhi

The Sharma family was paying for Netflix (₹649), Amazon Prime (₹1,499/year ≈ ₹125/month), Hotstar (₹299), Spotify (₹119), and a gym membership (₹2,000) that no one used for 4 months. EZer's subscription tracker revealed they were spending ₹3,192 monthly. They cancelled the unused gym (saving ₹8,000 in 4 months) and downgraded to just Prime and Hotstar, saving ₹30,000 annually!

Mistake #4: No Emergency Fund

The Problem: Life is unpredictable. Medical emergencies, job loss, or urgent home repairs can happen anytime. According to LocalCircles' financial wellness report, only a small percentage of Indians have an adequate emergency fund, with household savings dropping to historic lows.

The Recommended Amount: Financial experts suggest 6 months of expenses. For someone spending ₹40,000 monthly, that's ₹2.4 lakhs.

"An emergency fund is not a luxury - it's a necessity. It's the difference between a crisis and an inconvenience."
— Financial Planning Wisdom

How EZer Fixes This: Set up an emergency fund goal, and EZer tracks your progress. Even ₹1,000 monthly contributions add up to ₹12,000 in a year!
